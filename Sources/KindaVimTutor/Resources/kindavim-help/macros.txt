## Why It's Not Supported Here
kindaVim is a macOS menu-bar app that layers Vim-style motions on top of native text fields. Macro recording requires an in-process editor state (a register stream + replay engine) that the system text fields don't expose. Until an editor-like surface is embedded, macros are out of scope for kindaVim.

## How It Works In Stock Vim
Type `q` followed by a register letter (`a`–`z`) in {{normal}} to start recording into that register. Perform your edits — every keystroke is captured. Press `q` again to stop.

Replay the recorded sequence with `@` followed by the same register letter. For example: `qa` → some edits → `q` → `@a` replays them. Prefix with a count for repetition: `5@a` replays the macro five times.

## Closest kindaVim Alternative
For repeated single-command edits, dot-repeat (`.`) is supported and covers the common "redo last change" case. It's not a full macro engine — no multi-step sequences — but for things like "delete this word, and the next, and the next" it's the idiomatic kindaVim flow.

## Canonical QA
Q: How do I record a macro in kindaVim?
A: kindaVim doesn't support macros. For single-step repeats, use `.` to replay your last change.
Unsupported: yes

Q: Why doesn't `q` start recording in kindaVim?
A: kindaVim doesn't implement macros. `q` is unbound in kindaVim's {{normal}} mode and has no effect.
Unsupported: yes
TerminalVim: In stock Vim, `q<letter>` starts recording keystrokes into that register, and `q` on its own stops. The feature needs an in-process editor state that macOS text fields don't expose, which is why kindaVim can't implement it.
Related: . — repeat the last change, the closest supported single-step equivalent

Q: How do I replay a macro?
A: kindaVim doesn't support macros, so there's nothing to replay.
Unsupported: yes
TerminalVim: In stock Vim, type `@` followed by the register letter you recorded into. Prefix with a count like `5@a` to replay five times.
Related: . — repeat the last change in kindaVim

Q: Can I chain commands without a macro?
A: Yes — for simple multi-step edits that you want to reuse, chain them into one change where possible (e.g. `ciwNewText<Esc>`), then repeat with `.`. `.` replays the entire change as a unit.
Related: . — repeat last change; cgn — change next search match (useful for search-then-repeat)

Q: How do I record and replay a find-and-replace across a file?
A: Without macros, the kindaVim idiom is: `/pattern<Enter>` to search, `cgn<replacement><Esc>` to change the first match, then `.` (repeat change) after each `n` (next match). It's not automated but it's predictable and visible.
Related: / — search; cgn — change next search match; . — repeat change
