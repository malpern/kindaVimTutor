## What It Repeats
`.` in {{normal}} replays your LAST CHANGE. A change is anything that modifies the document — inserts, deletes, replaces, etc. It doesn't record pure motions (`j`, `w`, `$`) or searches.

## Canonical QA
Q: What does `.` do?
A: Press `.` in {{normal}} to repeat your last change. If you just deleted a word with `dw`, `.` deletes another. If you appended "foo" with `afoo<Esc>`, `.` appends "foo" again. It's the single most powerful habit in Vim.
Related: u — undo; Ctrl-R — redo

Q: What counts as "a change" for `.`?
A: Anything that modifies the text: inserts (`i`, `a`, `o`), deletes (`d`, `x`), changes (`c`, `s`), replaces (`r`), and insert-mode typing. Pure motions and searches don't count — `.` is for edits only.
Related: u — undo a change

Q: Does `.` repeat motions like `w` or `$`?
A: No — motions alone don't change the document. `.` only repeats the last CHANGE. If the change included a motion as part of an operator (`dw`), the whole `dw` gets repeated, not just `w`.
Related: n — repeat last search

Q: How do I undo a `.` repeat?
A: Same as any other change — press `u` to undo. Each `.` counts as its own undo step, so you can step back through several repeats.
Related: u — undo; Ctrl-R — redo

Q: How is `.` different from macros?
A: `.` repeats ONE change — the most recent one. Macros (not supported in kindaVim) record a sequence of keystrokes and can replay many steps. For single-command repetition, `.` is simpler and always there.
Related: u — undo; q — macro record (not supported)

Q: Does `.` know how many times I repeated before?
A: No — it repeats the last change once. Prefix with a count to multiply: `5.` repeats the change five times.
Related: dw — counts work on most commands

Q: What's the "dot formula" for efficient edits?
A: The idiom is: pair a motion you can repeat (`/pattern`, `f{char}`, or a word motion) with a single-change operator (`cw`, `ciw`, `dw`). Make the change once, then walk through the document with `n.` (or `;.` / `,.`) — each `n` jumps to the next match, each `.` replays the change.
Related: n — repeat search; / — forward search; cw — change word

Q: Can I combine `.` with a motion?
A: Not directly — `.` only repeats the last CHANGE. You can use motions to GET somewhere first, then press `.` to repeat the change at the new cursor. Common flow: `cw new<Esc>`, navigate (`w`, `5j`, `/target<Enter>`), press `.`.
Related: cw — change word; n — repeat search
