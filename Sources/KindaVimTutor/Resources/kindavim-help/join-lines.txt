## Canonical QA
Q: How do I join two lines?
A: Press `J` (capital) in {{normal}}. The next line is appended to the current one, separated by a single space. Leading whitespace on the joined line is removed.
Related: dd — delete line; o — open new line below

Q: Does `J` remove all whitespace between the lines?
A: It collapses trailing-of-line-one + leading-of-line-two into a single space. So "foo   " joined to "    bar" becomes "foo bar" — one space in between regardless of what was there.
Related: gJ — join without inserting the space (stock Vim; behavior varies in kindaVim)

Q: How do I join multiple lines at once?
A: Prefix with a count: `3J` joins the current line plus the next two (so three lines become one). Works for any count.
Related: J — single join; V3j — select lines and combine

Q: What happens if I join a line with an empty line?
A: `J` on a line followed by a blank one appends the blank — result is the original line with a trailing space (where the blank line would have been).
Related: dd — delete blank line instead of joining

Q: How is `J` different from pressing Backspace at the start of the next line?
A: Both merge two lines. `J` runs from {{normal}} mode without a position change; Backspace needs you in {{insert}} at the exact spot. `J` inserts a separator space between the joined content by default (behavior depends on context and host app options); Backspace just concatenates directly.
Related: dd — delete line instead of joining
