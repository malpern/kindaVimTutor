## How Undo Works
Press `u` in {{normal}} to undo the last change. kindaVim forwards this into macOS's native edit history, so it usually maps to whatever Cmd-Z would have done.

## Redo
Press `Ctrl-R` in {{normal}} to redo an undone change. This is distinct from stock Vim's `.` repeat — redo walks FORWARD in the undo history; `.` replays your last *intentional* change.

## Canonical QA
Q: What does `u` do?
A: Press `u` in {{normal}} to undo the last change. kindaVim maps this onto macOS's native edit history, so it behaves similarly to Cmd-Z.
Related: Ctrl-R — redo a change you just undid; . — repeat the last change (a separate concept from undo)

Q: How do I undo?
A: Press `u` in {{normal}} to undo your most recent change. Press `u` again to walk further back through the edit history.
Related: Ctrl-R — step forward through the undo history; . — repeat the last change

Q: How do I redo?
A: Press `Ctrl-R` in {{normal}} to redo a change you just undid. It walks FORWARD in the undo history.
Related: u — undo the last change; . — repeat the last change (different from redo)

Q: What's the difference between `u` and `.`?
A: `u` undoes changes — it walks BACKWARD through your edit history. `.` repeats your last change — if you just pressed `dw`, `.` does another `dw`. They're opposite directions: one erases history, the other replays it.
Related: Ctrl-R — redo an undone change
