## What Works
- `~` in {{normal}} — toggles the case of the character under the cursor and advances one character.
- `~` in {{visual}} — toggles case of the selection.
- `U` in {{visual}} — uppercases the selection.
- `u` in {{visual}} — lowercases the selection.

## What Doesn't
- `g~`, `gU`, `gu` (operator forms) — unimplemented in {{normal}}.
- `gUiw`, `guiw`, `g~iw` (operator + inner-word) — unimplemented.
- `U` in {{normal}} (line-level undo) — unimplemented.

## Canonical QA
Q: How do I toggle the case of a character?
A: Press `~` (tilde) in {{normal}} to flip the case of the character under the cursor. Uppercase becomes lowercase and vice-versa. The cursor advances one character after.
Related: r — replace single character

Q: How do I uppercase a whole word?
A: kindaVim doesn't implement `gU` / `gUiw` in {{normal}}. Type `viwU` instead — select the inner word with `viw`, then press `U` in {{visual}} to uppercase.
Unsupported: yes
TerminalVim: In stock Vim, `gUiw` uppercases the inner word in one go. `gU{motion}` applies to any motion (`gU$`, `gUap`, etc.).
Related: viwU — visual workaround (kindaVim supports it); viw~ — toggle case via visual

Q: How do I lowercase a selection?
A: Enter {{visual}} with `v` or `V`, select the text, then press `u` (lowercase in visual). This is distinct from {{normal}} mode's `u` (undo).
Related: U — uppercase in visual; ~ — toggle in visual

Q: How do I toggle case of a whole word?
A: kindaVim doesn't implement `g~iw` in {{normal}}. Type `viw~` — select the inner word with `viw`, then `~` in {{visual}} toggles case of the selection.
Unsupported: yes
TerminalVim: In stock Vim, `g~iw` toggles the case of the inner word directly, without entering Visual mode.
Related: viw~ — visual workaround (kindaVim supports it); viwU — uppercase via visual

Q: Does `gU` work in kindaVim?
A: kindaVim doesn't implement `gU`, `gu`, or `g~` as operators in {{normal}}. The case operators only exist inside {{visual}} as `U`, `u`, `~`. Select first with `v`, `V`, or a text-object shortcut like `viw`, then apply.
Unsupported: yes
TerminalVim: In stock Vim, `gU{motion}` uppercases the covered text, `gu{motion}` lowercases, `g~{motion}` toggles. `gUiw` / `guiw` / `g~iw` are the inner-word forms — common muscle-memory edits.
Related: viwU — visual workaround; viw~ — visual toggle

Q: Can I use `~` with a count?
A: Yes — `5~` toggles the case of the next five characters, advancing the cursor five spots.
Related: viw~ — toggle case of a word via visual selection
