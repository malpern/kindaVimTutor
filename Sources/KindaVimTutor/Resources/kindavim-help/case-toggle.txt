## Canonical QA
Q: How do I toggle the case of a character?
A: Press `~` (tilde) in {{normal}} to flip the case of the character under the cursor. Uppercase becomes lowercase and vice-versa. The cursor advances one character after.
Related: r — replace single character

Q: How do I toggle case of a whole word?
A: Stock Vim supports `g~iw` (toggle inner word case). Support varies in kindaVim — if it doesn't respond, the reliable fallback is to select the word with `viw` and press `~` in {{visual}}.
Related: viw~ — visual-mode case toggle

Q: How do I uppercase a whole word?
A: `gUiw` in stock Vim (uppercase inner word). `gU`/`gu` operators can behave differently on native text fields. A reliable approach is `viwU` (visual + uppercase).
Related: gu — lowercase operator; ~ — toggle only

Q: How do I lowercase a selection?
A: Enter visual with `v` or `V`, select the text, then press `u` to lowercase it. (Note: this `u` is a visual-mode operator, different from {{normal}}'s undo `u`.)
Related: U — uppercase in visual; ~ — toggle case

Q: Can I use `~` with a count?
A: Yes — `5~` toggles the case of the next five characters, advancing the cursor five spots.
Related: viw~ — toggle case of a word via visual selection
