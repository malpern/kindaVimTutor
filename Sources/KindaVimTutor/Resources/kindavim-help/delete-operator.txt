## The Grammar
`d{motion}` deletes from the cursor to wherever the motion would land. Pair with anything you know:

- `dw` delete to start of next word
- `de` delete to end of current word
- `db` delete back a word
- `d$` or `D` delete to end of line
- `d0` delete back to column zero
- `dG` delete to the end of the document
- `dgg` delete to the top
- `dd` delete the whole current line (a special linewise shortcut)

## Canonical QA
Q: What's the difference between `dd` and `D`?
A: `dd` deletes the ENTIRE current line (including the newline). `D` deletes from the cursor to the END of the line — leaves the line itself in place as an empty line.
Related: d$ — same as D; cc — change entire line; S — substitute entire line

Q: How do I delete a whole line?
A: Press `dd` in {{normal}} to delete the current line. Prefix with a count for multiple: `5dd` deletes five lines starting from the current one.
Related: D — delete to end of line (doesn't remove line break); dj — delete current line + next

Q: How do I delete from the cursor to the end of the line?
A: Press `D` in {{normal}} — shortcut for `d$`. Leaves the current line as whatever was before the cursor.
Related: dd — delete the whole line; d$ — explicit form of D

Q: How do I delete multiple lines?
A: Prefix `dd` with a count. `3dd` deletes three lines. Alternatively `dG` deletes from the current line to the end of the document, and `dgg` deletes to the top.
Related: dj — delete current + next; d{count}j — delete N lines down

Q: How do I delete the character under the cursor?
A: Press `x` in {{normal}} — a shorthand for `dl` (delete one character right). Prefix with a count: `5x` deletes five characters.
Related: X — delete char BEFORE cursor; r — replace one char without deleting

Q: How do I delete back a word?
A: Press `db` in {{normal}} — delete operator + back-a-word motion. Deletes from the cursor back to the start of the previous word.
Related: dw — delete forward a word; dge — delete back to end of previous word

Q: What's the difference between `dw` and `de`?
A: `dw` deletes from the cursor through the end of the word AND the trailing whitespace. `de` stops before the whitespace, leaving the space in place. Use `de` to preserve spacing; `dw` to remove the whole word unit.
Related: daw — delete around word (includes surrounding space); diw — delete inner word only

Q: How do I delete from here to a specific character?
A: Combine `d` with `t{char}` or `f{char}`. `dt)` deletes up to (not including) the next `)`. `df)` deletes through the `)`. Great for removing arguments, values, etc.
Related: ct) — change instead of delete; yt) — yank instead

Q: Where does deleted text go?
A: Into the default unnamed register, the same place yanks go. Paste it back with `p` or `P`. kindaVim's register model is simplified — just the one default register.
Related: p — put after cursor; P — put before cursor; y — yank instead of delete

Q: How do I delete without it being "deleted" — just remove it?
A: In kindaVim, every delete also copies to the default register (same as Vim). There's no separate "delete-no-yank" option without named registers. If you want to keep the previous yank, do your edit first, then re-yank what you need.
Related: y — yank; "a — named register (not supported in kindaVim)
