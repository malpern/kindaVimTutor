## What It Does
Type `dw` in {{normal}} to delete from the cursor to the start of the next word. It combines the `d` operator with the `w` motion, which makes it a good test case for the whole Vim grammar idea.

## How To Use It In KindaVim
Put the cursor at the beginning of the word you want gone, then type `dw`. If you start in the middle of a word, KindaVim still follows the motion from the current cursor position, so the exact deleted range depends on where you start.

Counts work the same way conceptually: `d2w` deletes across two word motions. That makes `dw` a good bridge from one-off edits into repeatable text transformations.

## Difference From Stock Vim
The command shape is the same, but KindaVim is acting on macOS text fields rather than an editor buffer. That means host-app behavior can still affect the feel of word boundaries in edge cases, especially in rich-text controls.

## Canonical QA
Q: How do I delete a word?
A: Type `dw` in {{normal}} to delete from the cursor through the end of the next word. Combine with a count like `3dw` to delete three words in a row.
Related: diw — delete inside the word under the cursor; daw — delete around the word including its trailing space
Faster: Press `D` instead if you want to delete from the cursor to the end of the line rather than just one word.

Q: What's the difference between `dw` and `de`?
A: `dw` deletes from the cursor through the end of the word AND the trailing whitespace, so "foo  bar" becomes "bar". `de` deletes through the end of the word but stops before the whitespace, leaving "  bar". Use `de` when you want to keep spacing; `dw` when you're removing the whole word unit.
Related: daw — delete around the word including the spaces on either side; diw — delete only the word's letters, leaving surrounding spaces

Q: How do I delete two words at once?
A: Type `d2w` in {{normal}} to delete across two word motions. The count goes between the operator `d` and the motion `w`, and it works the same for any repeatable motion — `d3w`, `d4w`, etc.
Related: d2e — delete through the end of the next two words without consuming the final trailing space

Q: What if my cursor is in the middle of a word when I type `dw`?
A: `dw` deletes from wherever the cursor sits to the start of the next word, so you'll delete just the tail of the current word plus any whitespace before the next one. If you want to remove the whole word regardless of cursor position, use `diw` instead — it deletes the word the cursor is "inside," leaving any surrounding spaces.
Related: diw — delete the entire word under the cursor; daw — delete the word plus its surrounding whitespace

Q: Does `dw` work at the end of a line?
A: Yes. When there's no next word on the current line, `dw` deletes to the end of the current line. Stock Vim can cross line boundaries in some cases; kindaVim's behavior depends on the host text field and typically stays within the line. If you need to pull the next line up afterward, use `J`.
Related: D — delete from the cursor to end of line; J — join the next line onto the current one

Q: How do I delete just the letters of a word, not the surrounding spaces?
A: Press `diw` in {{normal}} — "delete inner word." Works from anywhere in the word, leaves both the leading and trailing whitespace intact. Pair it with `caw` or `daw` when you DO want to consume surrounding space.
Related: daw — delete around word (includes surrounding space); ciw — change inner word

Q: Why did `dw` leave extra spaces behind?
A: It probably didn't — `dw` specifically eats the trailing whitespace through the start of the next word. If you're seeing extra spaces, you may have used `de` (delete to end of word, preserves space) or `diw` (delete inner word, preserves both sides). Pick the one that matches your intent.
Related: de — preserves trailing space; diw — preserves surrounding space

Q: How do I delete the word to the left?
A: Press `db` in {{normal}} to delete back one word — from the cursor to the start of the previous word. Or `dB` for WORD granularity (skip across punctuation).
Related: b — back-a-word motion alone; dge — delete back to end of previous word

Q: What's the fastest way to delete a sentence?
A: kindaVim doesn't implement the sentence text objects `is` / `as`. For a sentence-sized delete, use motions: `d$` (delete to end of line), or visual-select with `v` + motion + `d`.
Unsupported: yes
TerminalVim: In stock Vim, `das` deletes a sentence including trailing whitespace; `dis` deletes the sentence text without the whitespace.
Related: d$ — delete to end of line; v — enter visual for freeform selection
