## The Grammar
`c{motion}` deletes the covered text and enters {{insert}}. Everything that works with `d` works with `c` — same grammar, different ending state.

- `cw` change to next word
- `ce` change to end of word
- `cc` change the whole line (clears it, cursor on empty line in insert)
- `c$` or `C` change to end of line
- `ciw` / `caw` change inner / around word

## Canonical QA
Q: What's the difference between `c` and `d`?
A: Both delete the covered text, but `c` then enters {{insert}} so you can retype immediately. `d` leaves you in {{normal}}. Reach for `c` when you know you're about to retype; `d` when you're just removing.
Related: cc — change line; dc — nothing (operators don't compose); x — delete one char

Q: How do I change a whole line?
A: Type `cc` in {{normal}} — deletes the line's contents, leaves the line itself in place, and drops you into {{insert}} with the cursor on the (now empty) line. Same visual outcome as `S`.
Related: S — identical to cc; dd — delete line without entering insert

Q: What's the difference between `cc` and `C`?
A: `cc` clears the WHOLE line. `C` clears only from the cursor to the end of the line — leaves text before the cursor intact. Same relationship as `dd` vs `D`.
Related: c$ — explicit form of C; dd — delete whole line

Q: How do I change from here to the end of the line?
A: Press `C` in {{normal}} — shortcut for `c$`. Deletes from cursor to end of line and enters {{insert}}.
Related: cc — change whole line; D — delete to end of line without entering insert

Q: What's the difference between `cw` and `ciw`?
A: `cw` changes from the CURSOR to the end of the word — if the cursor is in the middle of "foobar", `cw` only changes "bar". `ciw` always changes the WHOLE word, no matter where the cursor sits on it.
Related: caw — change around word (includes trailing space); dw — same cursor-position behavior but for delete

Q: Does `cw` include the trailing whitespace?
A: Interestingly no — `cw` is treated like `ce` by Vim. It deletes through the end of the word but leaves the trailing space (unlike `dw` which consumes it). This is a quirk of the operator, not a bug.
Related: ce — equivalent of cw in this quirk; caw — includes surrounding space

Q: How do I change a word regardless of cursor position?
A: Use `ciw` — "change inner word." Works from anywhere in the word, deletes just the letters, enters {{insert}}. The go-to for renaming a variable or retyping a token.
Related: caw — includes surrounding space; ci" — change inside string

Q: How do I change everything inside quotes?
A: `ci"` for double-quoted; `ci'` for single-quoted. Deletes the contents, leaves the quotes, enters {{insert}}. Essential for editing string literals.
Related: ca" — change including the quotes; di" — delete without entering insert

Q: How do I change to the next instance of a character?
A: `ct{char}` changes up to (not including) the next char; `cf{char}` changes through it. `ct)` → change to next `)`, leave paren in place, enter insert. `cf)` → change through the paren.
Related: dt — delete version; yt — yank version

Q: Is `s` the same as `cl`?
A: Yes. `s` (substitute character) is a shortcut for "change one character to the right": delete the character under the cursor and enter insert. `cl` is the spelled-out version.
Related: S — shortcut for cc; r — replace char without entering insert
