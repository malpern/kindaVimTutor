## Three Steps
1. Press `v` in {{normal}} to start a character-wise selection, or `V` for line-wise.
2. Use any motion to extend the selection: `w`, `e`, `$`, `f)`, `iw`, `2j`, etc.
3. Press an operator (`d`, `c`, `y`) to act on the selection. Or press `Esc` to cancel.

## Canonical QA
Q: How do I enter visual mode?
A: Press `v` in {{normal}} to start a character-wise visual selection. Move with any motion to extend it. Press `Esc` to cancel, or an operator (`d`, `c`, `y`) to act on it.
Related: V — linewise visual; Esc — cancel

Q: What's the difference between `v` and `V`?
A: `v` is CHARACTERWISE — selection starts at a single character and grows character by character. `V` is LINEWISE — selection is always whole lines, no matter how far you move within a line.
Related: Esc — return to normal

Q: How do I delete a visual selection?
A: Press `d` (or `x`) while the selection is active. The selected text is deleted and you return to {{normal}}. The deleted text lands in the default register; paste it back with `p`.
Related: c — change (delete + enter insert); y — yank (copy without deleting)

Q: How do I yank a visual selection?
A: Make the selection with `v` or `V`, then press `y`. The text goes to the default register and visual mode exits. Cursor position after yank depends on the direction of the selection — don't rely on it being at any specific end.
Related: d — delete version; p — paste

Q: How do I change a visual selection?
A: Make the selection, then press `c`. The selected text is deleted and you enter {{insert}} ready to retype.
Related: d — delete only; s — substitute one character

Q: Can I extend a visual selection word by word?
A: Yes — while in visual mode, `w` extends by one word, `b` extends backward, `e` to end of word. Any motion works; the selection grows or shrinks to match.
Related: $ — extend to end of line; 0 — extend to start

Q: How do I select a whole line?
A: Press `V` (capital) in {{normal}} to enter linewise visual. The current line is selected. Press `j` / `k` to extend to adjacent lines.
Related: v — characterwise; yy — yank line without visual

Q: How do I select text inside parens in visual mode?
A: Type `vib` in {{normal}} — "visual inside block." kindaVim uses the `b` alias for the `()` text object, so `vib` selects inside parens, `vab` selects them too. Same grammar for `viB` / `vaB` on braces.
Related: viw — select inner word; vi" — select inside quotes; vib — select inside parens

Q: How do I cancel a visual selection?
A: Press `Esc` — returns to {{normal}} with no edit. The cursor ends up at the last position of the selection.
Related: v — re-enter visual

Q: How do I re-select the last visual selection?
A: kindaVim doesn't implement `gv`. To re-visit an area, manually re-enter visual with `v` or `V` and navigate back to it — your cursor position from before the selection is often a reasonable anchor.
Unsupported: yes
TerminalVim: In stock Vim, `gv` restores the bounds of your last visual selection exactly (start, end, and mode — characterwise or linewise).
Related: v — enter visual; V — enter linewise visual

Q: Does visual mode support counts?
A: Yes — in visual mode, a count before a motion multiplies it. `v3w` selects from the cursor forward by three words. The count applies to the motion, not the operator.
Related: w — counts work on motions outside visual too

Q: How do I select from here to the end of the line?
A: Press `v`, then `$` — character-wise selection to end of line. Or in linewise visual (`V`), the current line is selected and you can extend.
Related: V — linewise from the start
