## The Grammar
Text objects are used AFTER an operator (`d`, `c`, `y`). The form is:

`{operator}{i or a}{object}`

- `i` stands for **inner** ‚Äî the object's contents, excluding delimiters/surrounding space.
- `a` stands for **around** ‚Äî the contents INCLUDING delimiters or trailing whitespace.

## The Objects (kindaVim-supported)
- `w` / `W` ‚Äî word / WORD
- `(` `)` `b` ‚Äî parenthesized expression
- `{` `}` `B` ‚Äî brace-delimited block
- `[` `]` ‚Äî bracketed block
- `"` ‚Äî double-quoted string
- `'` ‚Äî single-quoted string

## Canonical QA
Q: What's the difference between `iw` and `aw`?
A: `iw` is the word WITHOUT surrounding whitespace ‚Äî just the letters. `aw` is the word AROUND ‚Äî includes the trailing (or leading) space. `diw` on the middle word of "foo bar baz" leaves "foo  baz" (double space); `daw` leaves "foo baz".
Related: ciw ‚Äî change inner word; caw ‚Äî change around word; yiw ‚Äî yank inner word

Q: How do I delete the word the cursor is on?
A: Press `diw` in {{normal}} ‚Äî works no matter where in the word the cursor sits. `diw` deletes inner word (just letters); `daw` deletes around word (letters + one surrounding space).
Related: dw ‚Äî delete from cursor forward to next word (cursor-position-dependent); daw ‚Äî delete word + surrounding space

Q: How do I change the word the cursor is on?
A: Press `ciw` to delete the inner word and drop straight into {{insert}}. Doesn't matter where in the word the cursor sits ‚Äî `ciw` replaces the whole thing.
Related: caw ‚Äî change around word; cw ‚Äî change from cursor to end of word

Q: How do I yank a quoted string?
A: With the cursor on the line containing the quoted string, type `yi"` in {{normal}} to yank the contents (excluding the quotes) or `ya"` to include the quotes. Same pattern with `yi'` / `ya'` for single-quoted strings.
Related: di" ‚Äî delete inside quotes; ci" ‚Äî change inside quotes

Q: How do I delete everything inside parentheses?
A: `di(` or `di)` ‚Äî both work, same result. The cursor can be anywhere on the line containing the parens. `da(` or `da)` deletes including the parens themselves.
Related: ci( ‚Äî change inside parens; c% ‚Äî change to matching bracket (stock Vim only)

Q: Does `i(` work on any type of bracket?
A: Each bracket type has its own text object: `i(`/`i)` for `()`, `i[`/`i]` for `[]`, `i{`/`i}` for `{}`. There's also `iB` as a shortcut for `i{`. Use the one matching the delimiters you want.
Related: ib ‚Äî shortcut for i(; iB ‚Äî shortcut for i{

Q: What does the cursor need to be on for `di(` to work?
A: When the cursor is inside a `()` pair, `di(` operates on that pair. In stock Vim, if the cursor is outside any parens, `di(` scans forward to find the next `()` block. In kindaVim the safe rule is to position the cursor inside the parens first ‚Äî host-app behavior varies otherwise.
Related: di) ‚Äî identical behavior; ci( ‚Äî change inside

Q: What's the difference between `i"` and `a"`?
A: `i"` gives you the string CONTENTS (between the quotes, excluding them). `a"` includes the quotes themselves. So on `"hello"`: `di"` leaves `""`; `da"` leaves nothing.
Related: di' ‚Äî single-quoted version; ci" ‚Äî change inside quotes

Q: How do I change the contents of a string without removing the quotes?
A: `ci"` in {{normal}} ‚Äî deletes just the contents and drops you into {{insert}}, with the quotes preserved. Reach for this any time you're retyping a string literal.
Related: ci' ‚Äî single-quote version; diw ‚Äî for unquoted word

Q: How do I select text using a text object?
A: In {{visual}} mode, apply a text object to extend the selection. `viw` enters visual, then selects the inner word. `vi(` selects everything inside parens. Press an operator to act on the selection or `Esc` to cancel.
Related: v ‚Äî enter visual mode first; V ‚Äî linewise visual

Q: What's the difference between `di(` and `di)`?
A: None ‚Äî both refer to the same parenthesized text object. `b` is also a shortcut for `(` / `)` (so `dib` = `di(`). Pick whichever is ergonomic.
Related: dib ‚Äî shorthand; diB ‚Äî shorthand for di{

Q: Can I use a count with text objects?
A: Stock Vim does accept counts with many text objects ‚Äî e.g. `d2iw` or `d2aw` to delete across two words, and counts on block text objects expand to the next enclosing pair. kindaVim's support for counts on text objects is uneven, so prefer explicit motion counts (`d3w`) when you need consistent behavior.
Related: d3w ‚Äî delete across three words; daw ‚Äî delete one word with surrounding space

Q: How do I delete to the matching bracket?
A: `di{` or `di}` deletes inside the enclosing braces. If you want to include the braces, use `da{`. To match opening to closing without using a text object, stock Vim's `%` motion works but text objects are usually clearer.
Related: da{ ‚Äî delete braces included; di} ‚Äî same inside

Q: My cursor is after the word. Will `diw` still work?
A: `diw` operates on the word the cursor is ON or the nearest word if the cursor is on whitespace between words. If the cursor is truly past all content, nothing matches.
Related: daw ‚Äî similar rules but includes trailing space

Q: Which text object is the most useful one to learn first?
A: `ciw` and `ci"`. "Change inner word" rewrites a variable or identifier from anywhere on it; "change inner string" retypes a literal without touching the quotes. These two cover a huge share of everyday edits.
Related: ci( ‚Äî change inside parens; yiw ‚Äî yank inner word
