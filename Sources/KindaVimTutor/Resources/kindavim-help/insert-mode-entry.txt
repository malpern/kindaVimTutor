## Six Ways In
Every insert-mode entry puts you in {{insert}} but at a different cursor position:

- `i` — insert BEFORE the cursor
- `I` — insert at the first non-blank character of the line
- `a` — append AFTER the cursor
- `A` — append at the end of the line
- `o` — open a new line BELOW and start inserting
- `O` — open a new line ABOVE and start inserting

Pick the one that lands where you want to type. Often one capital letter saves three motions.

## When To Use Which
If you're already on the character you want to type before, `i`. If you want to type after (most common at end of word), `a`. If you want the start of the line, `I` is faster than `0i`. End of the line, `A` beats `$a`. New blank line, `o` or `O` — no need to position first.

## Canonical QA
Q: How do I start typing in kindaVim?
A: Press `i` in {{normal}} to enter {{insert}} before the cursor. Press `Esc` when you're done typing to return to {{normal}}.
Related: a — enter insert AFTER the cursor; o — open a new line below and insert; I — insert at the first non-blank of the line

Q: What's the difference between `i` and `a`?
A: `i` inserts BEFORE the cursor character; `a` inserts AFTER it. If the cursor is on the `l` in "world" and you press `a`, you type between the `l` and the `d`. With `i`, you type between the `r` and the `l`.
Related: I — insert at line start; A — append at line end

Q: How do I append to the end of a line?
A: Press `A` in {{normal}} to jump to the end of the line AND enter {{insert}} in one step. It's a shortcut for `$a` — faster when you want to keep typing at the end.
Related: I — the mirror-image shortcut for line start; a — append one char after cursor; o — open next line below

Q: How do I insert at the beginning of a line?
A: Press `I` (capital) in {{normal}} to jump to the first non-blank character AND enter {{insert}} in one step. Shortcut for `^i`.
Related: A — append at end of line; i — insert at exact cursor position

Q: How do I start a new line while in normal mode?
A: Press `o` in {{normal}} to open a new line BELOW the current line and enter {{insert}}. Press `O` (capital) to open a new line ABOVE. Both drop you into {{insert}} ready to type.
Related: a — append after cursor; A — append at end of current line

Q: What's the difference between `o` and `O`?
A: `o` opens a new line BELOW the current line and enters {{insert}} on it. `O` opens a new line ABOVE the current line instead. Both leave you in {{insert}} with the cursor on the fresh line.
Related: a — append after cursor; Esc — leave {{insert}} back to {{normal}}

Q: How do I get back to normal mode?
A: Press `Esc` in {{insert}} to return to {{normal}}. Once there, keystrokes become commands again instead of typed characters.
Related: i — re-enter insert; . — repeat the last change

Q: What does `s` do?
A: Press `s` in {{normal}} to delete the character under the cursor AND enter {{insert}} — same as `xi` in one step. Useful for replacing a single character with possibly multiple characters.
Related: r — replace one character without entering insert; S — substitute the entire line

Q: What does `S` do?
A: Press `S` in {{normal}} to delete the whole current line AND enter {{insert}} on an empty line. Same as `cc`.
Related: cc — equivalent to S; dd — delete line without entering insert

Q: How do I add text without erasing what's there?
A: Any of `i`, `I`, `a`, `A`, `o`, `O` keep existing text and let you insert new text at the chosen position. Only `s`, `S`, `r`, and change operators like `cw` delete first.
Related: cw — change a word (delete + insert); r — replace one character

Q: My cursor disappeared after typing — am I still in insert mode?
A: Check the mode indicator in the top-right of kindaVim Tutor. If it shows {{insert}}, you're still in insert mode. Press `Esc` to get back to {{normal}} where motions like `hjkl` work again.
Related: Esc — return to normal mode

Q: How do I undo accidentally entering insert mode?
A: Press `Esc` to return to {{normal}} first, then `u` to undo anything you typed. Insert mode itself doesn't record as a change — only the text you entered while there.
Related: u — undo; Ctrl-R — redo
